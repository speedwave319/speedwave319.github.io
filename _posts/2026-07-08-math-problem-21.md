Let  

$$
M =
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
\lambda & b & a \\
\mu & c & -b
\end{pmatrix}
$$


,where $b^2 + ac = 1$. Using mathematical induction, prove that   

$$
M^{2n} =
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
n[\lambda(1+b) + \mu a] & 1 & 0 \\
n[\lambda c + \mu(1-b)] & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

for all positive integers $n$.

(b) Using the result of (a), or otherwise, evaluate

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
-2 & 3 & 2 \\
1 & -4 & -3
\end{pmatrix}^{2000}.
$$

($4+2$ marks)
