(a) let $a$ be a non-zero constant. Prove that $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1} x^2 e^{ax}\ dx = \frac{(a^2-2a+2)e^a -2}{a^3}$ .

(b) Using (a) and integration by substitution, evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{e -1} x(\ln (1+x))^2\ dx $.

(c) Evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} (\ln(1+(e-1) \cos {x}))^2 \sin 2x \ dx $.

(d) Evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\pi} (\ln(1+(e-1) \sin x))^2 \sin 2x \ dx $.
