(a) let $a$ be a positive constant. 

(i) Express $\displaystyle \int x\sqrt{x^2 +a}\ dx$ in terms of $a$.

(ii) Prove that $\displaystyle \int (\frac{1}{x+ \sqrt{x^2 +a}})^2\ dx = \frac{2x^3}{3a^2} - \frac{2(x^2 +a)^\frac{3}{2}}{3a^2} + \frac{x}{a}+ C$, where $C$ is a constant. 

(b) Define $\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{1}{x+ \sqrt{x^2 +a}}$ for all $x \geq 0$. Denote the graph of $y=f(x)$ by $G$. Let $R$ be the region bounded by $G$, the $x4-axis and the straight line $x=2$. Find the volume of the solid of revolution generated by revolving R about the x-axis.
