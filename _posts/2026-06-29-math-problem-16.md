(a) Evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1} \frac{1}{x^2+2x+5}\ dx$.
  
(b)(i) Let $\displaystyle 0 \leq \theta \leq \frac{\pi}{4}$. Prove that $\displaystyle \frac{2\tan\theta}{1+\tan^2\theta} = \sin2\theta$ and $\displaystyle \frac{1-\tan^2\theta}{1+\tan^2\theta} = \cos2\theta$.

(b)(ii) Using the substitution $t = \tan\theta$, evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{1}{\sin2\theta +2\cos2\theta +3}\ d\theta$.

(c) Prove that $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{1}{\sin2\theta +2\cos2\theta +3}\ d\theta = \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{1}{\cos2\theta +2\sin2\theta +3}\ d\theta$.

(d) Evaluate $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{3(\sin2\theta+ \cos2\theta +2)}{(\sin2\theta +2\cos2\theta +3)(\cos2\theta +2\sin2\theta +3)}\ d\theta$.

($3+(2+3)+2+4$ marks)
